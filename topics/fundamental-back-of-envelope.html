Math that fits in a whiteboard.

Back-of-envelope estimation is the skill that turns "design Twitter" into "your design needs 6k QPS of writes and 600k of reads." Two minutes of math anchors every subsequent decision. The trick: memorise a handful of numbers; convert everything else.

The numbers to memorise

1 byte/char. ~10⁵ seconds per day. ~10⁹ in a year. DRAM ns; SSD μs; cross-region 100 ms.

The shape

Users → events/user → events/sec → QPS · storage · bandwidth. Convert each into a budget.

01Why estimate.

Architectural choices follow from numbers. "100M users" tells you almost nothing; "6k writes/sec" tells you whether you can fit on one Postgres or need sharding. The estimation step is the bridge from product requirement to engineering decision. Skipping it leaves the decision unanchored.

The interview signal is that you can estimate, not that your numbers are perfect. "About 5k QPS" is a fine answer; "I have no idea" isn't.

"The number is the design. Without it you're just naming boxes."

03A worked example — Twitter.

500M DAU. Average user writes 1 tweet/day, reads 100/day.

  • Writes/day: 500M × 1 = 5 × 10⁸ tweets/day
  • Writes/sec: 5 × 10⁸ ÷ 10⁵ = 5 × 10³ ≈ 5k tweets/sec
  • Reads/sec: 100× writes ≈ 500k QPS
  • Storage/day: 5 × 10⁸ × 1 KB = 500 GB/day
  • Storage/year: 500 GB × 365 = ~180 TB/year
  • Bandwidth (reads): 500k QPS × ~1 KB = ~500 MB/sec = ~4 Gbps

Conclusion in 90 seconds: needs sharded storage, aggressive caching (you cannot hit DB at 500k QPS), CDN for media. Numbers chose the design.

The relevant rule of thumb: cross-network is ~1000× memory. Disk is in between. Any design that hits the network on the hot path has paid that tax.

05Tips.

Round generously

2.78 × 10⁵ becomes "about 300k." The interviewer doesn't care about precision; they care that you can compute.

Read/write ratios are the unlock

Most products are read-heavy (100:1 typical for social, 10:1 for SaaS, 1:1 for chat). Naming the ratio early simplifies everything that follows.

Peak ≈ 4× average

Daily peak is roughly 4× the mean. If average is 5k QPS, design for 20k peak.

Don't estimate things that don't matter

If the answer makes no architectural decision turn, skip it. Estimation serves design choice.

"In system design interviews, the people who can't do mental math sound less senior. That's the bar."
